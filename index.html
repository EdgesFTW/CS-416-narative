CS 416 Narative Visualization

An analysis of grade inflation through from 2010 to 2023. Dataset taken from Wade Fagen-Ulmschneider's public GPA dataset at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, gathered through FOIA requests.

Grade inflation is the phenomenon that occurs overtime as students as a whole are awarded higher and higher grades. Here, I will attempt to highlight the various differences between the academic units (Grainger, Gies, ACES, ect.) and how they experience grade inflation over the last decade and a half.

To start, begin scrolling!

We first highlight the University's overall gpa over the last decade and a half. We can very clearly see a strong trend over time. The overall, university wide gpa has been steadily climbing since 2010 and the rate that it has been climbing has increased as time went on. Specifically, in 2020, the first full year of covid, the campus wide average gpa skyrockets for a year. It then drops back down a little bit when we return to inperson classes, however it continues to rise afterwards.

We then zoom in on the largest academic units Grainger, LAS, and Gies, to better understand how a students from each academic unit experiance grade inflation. We see a similar trend to the average, we see the same spikes during covid, but we also see how Gies is not accelerating the same as LAS and Grainger after COVID.

We should highlight more than just the average gpa of these academic units. We can take the minimum class gpa each year to get a better idea of the 'worst case'. Because we are taking the gpa of a single class each year, this is a significantly more volatile metric than taking the average over the entier academic unit.

While the metric may be more sensitive, we get a better idea of how a student may feel as students tend to have more strong emotions from courses they do not do well in.

The following streamchart is used to highlight realative differences between the GPAs of academic units every year. The minimum each year is subtracted off of all elements to better highlight the differences.

The height of the streamchart can be though of as a measure of the variance of GPAs across academic units. From this, we can see that the different academic unit's average gpa did not vary significantly early on in 2010 and 2011, but overtime their averages have began to drift. This goes to show that not every academic unit experiances grade inflation at the same rate.
